DEBRIEFING:

What is this study about?

  • This study examines how people resolve sentences with two meanings.

    • For example: "I saw the man with the telescope" could mean...

      • "I used the telescope to see the man"

      • "I saw the man that has the telescope"

  • These two interpretations are possible because a telescope can be used to see things AND it can be something that people have.

  • Now, if we change the object to a donut. We typically only get the meaning where the man has the donut, rather than using a donut to see things.  

    • For example: "I saw the man with the donut"

      • "I used the donut to see the man" (not so great)

      • "I saw the man that has the donut" (most likely)

  • We are investigating how speed of spoken sentences influences our ability to use information about the object (e.g. telescopes, donuts) to guide our interpretations. 

How is the study conducted?

In this task, you saw 18 sentences of varying speeds. You listened to sentences like "Jeff saw the man with the telescope" and then answered questions. The questions asked "What did Jeff see?" which could either be "the man" or "the man with the telescope". We are looking to see how speed affects which answer you provided to the questions.
