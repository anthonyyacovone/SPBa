In this experiment, you will listen to sentences and then answer questions about what you heard.

The context of the sentences: Children playing with toy animals!

Each sentence will describe something that a child is acting out with a toy animal. For example, you may hear, "Jane loves to make pastries with the moose!" In this sentence, you could imagine Jane making fake treats with her moose stuffed animal. 

Another example: "The cat really likes pizza for breakfast." We may ask you a question like...

Q: Who really likes pizza for breakfast?  A: The cat! 
Q: What does the cat really like?  A: Pizza for breakfast! 
Q: What does the cat like for breakfast?  A: Pizza! 

After listening to the sentence, you will hit continue to go to the question screen. You will have as much time as needed to fully read the question and provide your answer. Simply type your answer into the input box and then hit 'Enter'.

It will sound like listening to an audiobook or a video at 2 times the original rate. 

You will hear practice sentences in order to get used to the faster speeds.
